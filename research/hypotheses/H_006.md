# Hypothesis: H_006

## ID
H_006

## Logic
At known strategy venues, disagreement between bookmaker closing probability and Betfair exchange probability may identify the stronger price signal.

The current strategy treats venue edge as a direct comparison between historical venue performance and one market price at a time. A tighter hypothesis is that when `bk_implied_home` and `bf_implied_home` materially diverge on the same match, the side of that disagreement may contain additional information about mispricing strength.

This stays inside the current schema and does not require new data sources. It is also a clean Ideator candidate because it tests interaction between an established signal class:
- venue bias
- and an already available market comparison:
- bookmaker vs exchange implied probability

## Success Metric
- Restrict to matches where both bookmaker and Betfair implied probabilities are available.
- Test whether probability disagreement at strategy venues is associated with actual outcome in a direction that improves on the base venue-bias rule.
- Require explicit reporting of sample size by venue and by disagreement bucket.
- Require `p_value < 0.05` for any directional claim.
- Report ROI impact of a simple disagreement filter layered onto the existing venue strategy.
- Treat any result as exploratory if the backtest is not leakage-safe or is purely in-sample.
