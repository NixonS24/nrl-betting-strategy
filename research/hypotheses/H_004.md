# Hypothesis: H_004

## ID
H_004

## Logic
The current AAMI Park venue edge may not be a venue effect in the broad sense. It may instead be mostly or entirely a **Melbourne Storm home-team effect** expressed through a venue that is rarely neutral and rarely hosts other true home sides.

If that is true, then treating AAMI Park as a generic venue signal could overstate portability and hide the real mechanism behind the edge. The hypothesis should test whether the observed home win advantage at `venue_name == 'AAMI Park'` remains meaningful once the sample is framed explicitly around `home_team`.

This is a robustness check on the flagship venue edge, not an attempt to create a more complex model.

## Variables
- `venue_name`
- `home_team`
- `away_team`
- `season`
- `result`
- `bk_home_close`
- `bk_implied_home`
- `bf_home_open`
- `bf_implied_home`

## Success Metric
- Confirm whether AAMI Park qualifying matches are overwhelmingly or exclusively Melbourne Storm home matches.
- Compare actual home win rate at `venue_name == 'AAMI Park'` against market-implied home probability using the available bookmaker and/or Betfair subsets.
- Require explicit reporting of sample composition by `home_team`.
- Require `p_value < 0.05` for any claim that the edge is venue-specific rather than team-specific.
- If the sample is effectively all Melbourne Storm home matches, classify the current result as a team-linked venue edge rather than a generic venue effect.
