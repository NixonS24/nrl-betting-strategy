# Hypothesis: H_010

## ID
H_010

## Logic
The near-flip bucket may be a better market-structure test than the previously rejected 45–55% home-probability claim.

The earlier 45–55% calibration idea was rejected as a weak, narrow, and underpowered bucket. A tighter hypothesis is to test **near-flip matches**:
- games where the market is close enough to a side-switch that small repricing may reveal real information
- but structured prospectively so the test does not collapse into the same broad coin-flip claim

This should be treated as a dedicated follow-up to the earlier calibration question, not as a re-run of it.

## Success Metric
- Define a near-flip bucket explicitly before testing and report the exact rule used.
- Use a prospective-style design where the bucket is evaluated as a forward decision rule, not just a retrospective calibration slice.
- Test whether near-flip matches show directional value stronger than the earlier rejected 45–55% home-probability bucket.
- Require explicit reporting of sample size, realised win rate, implied win rate, and bucket composition.
- Require `p_value < 0.05` for any directional claim.
